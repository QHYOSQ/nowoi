What is URL encoding?

URL encoding is the process of converting characters into a format that can be transmitted over the Internet. It replaces unsafe ASCII characters with a "%" followed by two hexadecimal digits.

When should I use URL encoding?

URL encoding is necessary when you need to include special characters in a URL, such as spaces, ampersands, or other non-ASCII characters. It's commonly used in query parameters and form data.
