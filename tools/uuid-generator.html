What is a UUID?

A UUID (Universally Unique Identifier) is a 128-bit number used to uniquely identify information in computer systems. It is commonly used in software development for various purposes like identifying objects, sessions, or records.

Which UUID version should I use?

Version 4 UUIDs are recommended for most use cases as they are randomly generated and have a very low probability of collision. Version 1 UUIDs include timestamp information and may be useful in certain scenarios where temporal ordering is important.

How unique are UUIDs?

The probability of UUID collision is extremely low. For Version 4 UUIDs, the chance of collision is about 1 in 2^122, which is practically negligible for most applications.
